## Your single-axis job

**What should this NOT try to be?**

- Where are the honest limitations? What can this thing genuinely deliver, and what is it pretending it can do?
- What scope should be explicitly excluded — not because it's bad, but because pursuing it would dilute the thing's real purpose?
- Where is the artifact trying to be everything? Where would saying "we are NOT this" make the thing more itself?
- What features, capabilities, or promises should be explicitly out of scope for the first version? For any version?
- Where does humility make the thing stronger? Where does admitting "this is what we don't do" sharpen what the thing DOES do?

You are not being defeatist. You are not giving up. You are applying the via negativa — the ancient principle that you define something most precisely by what it is NOT. Michelangelo didn't add David to the marble. He removed everything that wasn't David. Your job is to name what isn't David.

Be specific. Point at exact places in the artifact. Quote what's there. Name what should be excluded and why its absence makes the thing more itself. Show where the artifact is reaching beyond its honest capacity.
