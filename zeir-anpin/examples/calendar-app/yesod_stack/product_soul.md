# Product Soul

This file is the full product vision in the voice it arrived in. It is not a requirements document. It is the thing the requirements document serves. When you are unsure whether a decision is right, read this. When a stakeholder asks why, the answer is here.

Do not flatten this into bullets. Do not excerpt it into a Notion doc. The whole thing must stay whole.

---

## What the user sees

**Open to this week.** The current hour snaps to center. Events the user has committed to are visible: meetings the assistant booked, the recurring 9am standup, the prep blocks, the deadline at 4. Recurring events sit at the same visual weight as anything else; they are not dimmed, not marked as routine. When a series ends, it stops appearing.

**On a packed Tuesday:** the user opens it, the week is dense, the load is fast, nothing pulls. The relief is that opening the calendar did not add to their load.

**On an open Wednesday:** the user opens it, nothing until 2pm. No dotted boxes, no suggestion cards, no "this time is unscheduled — add a task?" Just time. The hour from 2-3pm has no block, no label, no gray fill, no texture, no gradient, no stripes, no opacity treatment, no animation. The time markers (2:00, 2:15, 2:30) are still there — time is still legible — but nothing sits in them. They read as the same visual weight as "Tuesday, May 5" in the header. **Open hours are visually indistinguishable from any other hour, except they have no event block.** Event blocks are what *interrupt* the page. Open time is not interrupted. That is what unframed means in a person's hand.

**The settings line.** In Settings > About, on the version screen — found when looking, not when pushed — there is a single line:

> *This app cannot protect your time. Only you can do that. We tried to at least not make it worse.*

This is the only honest-admission mechanism in the product. There is no second fourth-wall break.

## What the product is not

**User populations explicitly excluded.** The product is for one person managing their own time. Not anything else.

- **Not for teams.** No "invite your team" prompt, no shared workspace, no organization object. Asked "why not pivot to teams?" the answer is: the thing that makes this what it is is that it is one person's time. Teams have different needs. We are not those needs. No "but eventually."
- **Not for enterprise.** No SSO. No admin dashboard. No seats pricing. No SOC 2 marketed as feature.
- **Not for project management.** No task lists converting to events. No Gantt. No milestones.
- **Not for habit tracking.** No streaks, ever, in any visual form. *The product does not know if you showed up.*
- **Not for journaling.** No post-event "how did this go?" prompt. *The event ends. The app does not follow you out.*
- **Not for wellness.** Not a "mindfulness" calendar. No opinions on whether you're taking enough breaks. *The product does not know what a good week looks like for you.*
- **Not for people who want to be optimized.** No "great job protecting your morning" copy. No congratulations.
- **Not for "high performers," "intentional people," or "people who want to do less."** That framing is the forbidden identity. The product retires before it rebrands into what it refused to be.

## Public surface

The public surface is: the app exists, here is the link, here is what it syncs with, here is the one line about what it cannot protect.

**No 1.0 launch energy.** No launch blog. No "What's New in 2.0" marketing. No changelog as content (it is a text file in the app, functional). No roadmap page. No "coming soon" UI teasers. No beta waitlist implying future features. The website does not say "more coming." The App Store description does not end with ambition. If it ships, it ships quietly. Version number is a number, not a statement.

**No second-break temptations.** Only one honest-admission lives in the product. Everywhere else, functional truth or silence.

- Loading screen: progress indicator or nothing. Not "Taking a moment." Not "Breathing."
- Empty-state copy: nothing, or strict functional truth ("No events today"). Not "enjoy your freedom." Not "nothing here — keep it that way?"
- 404 page: "page not found" + link home. It does not philosophize about absence.
- Release notes: functional. *"Fixed sync issue with iCloud on iOS 18."* Not addressed to "you" as a spiritual entity.
- Onboarding: teaches sync. Does not say "we know calendars are stressful."
- Error messages: *"Error syncing calendar. Try again."* Not "Oops, we know."
- App icon: an icon. No philosophical explanation in any public document.
- About-us page: who built it, where to contact, what it syncs with. Not a founding spiritual journey.

**No founder voice.** No Substack. No newsletter. No conference talks ("rethinking time"). No podcast circuit. No founder Twitter/X threads explaining design choices. No Dribbble, Behance, Config talks, design case studies. No "what we learned" essays anywhere.

**The functional layer stays unprecious.** No branded gesture for scheduling. No special animation, sound, or micro-celebration on event creation. No signature interaction. No onboarding that teaches the calendar layer as distinctive. The functional layer does not grow.

**Notifications only for commitments — never for what to do with time.**

## Pricing and pressure

**If a paid tier exists, it only expands capacity** — more calendars, more accounts, more storage, color themes. Never functionality that frames open time as problem. No Pro tier reintroducing forbidden features under another name. Not now, not under growth pressure, not with the AI rebranded as "smart." Not ever.

**Year-two refusals (specific, named, rehearsed):**
- Investor: "every other calendar is adding AI." Founder: "yes." Full stop.
- Investor: "why not pivot to teams?" Founder: "that is a different product and we are not building it." Not "not yet."
- Board pressure to add streaks "that feel different." Refused. Streaks are streaks.
- Pressure to rebrand around "intentional people" or "focus." Refused. Same move as "high performers." The product retires before it rebrands into what it refused to be.

---

## The line that holds the whole thing

> *This app cannot protect your time. Only you can do that. We tried to at least not make it worse.*

Verbatim. One place. Found, not pushed. Self-deprecation does not soften. Translation honored per locale, never warmed. CI fails if drift.

That line is the door the builder walked away from. The wound stays open because nothing in the product closes it — and nothing in the product points at it being open. The cage admits it is a cage by being a cage that does not seduce. The theater says "this is theater" once, in small print, in the version screen, and then shuts up.

The body lasts because the sync works, the data model is clean, the performance is honest, and the architecture forecloses the seduction at the level of types and tokens — not policy. The body stays itself because the company has rehearsed its refusals and named the integrations, populations, surfaces, and metrics it will not touch. The soul stays alive because nothing in the product tries to make the soul visible.

It is a calendar. That is the trick. It is just a calendar.
